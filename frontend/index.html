Who Are We and What We Built

We are a team of Imperial College Computing students who gathered and analysed data generated for 200+ countries around the world.

Using our statistical model, we can predict what the number of coronavirus cases will be in 50 days for most countries across the globe.


How We Built It

We built our frontend using HTML and JavaScript, the backend in C, and the interface between the two using WebAssembly.

For our statistical analysis, we used a logistic growth model as it is has been widely used to model previous pandemics; it reflects the exponential growth during the early stages of an epidemic and tends towards a limit that represents the later stages. We calculated the values of our parameters to best fit existing data and utilised them to predict future cases.

However, due to inaccuracies in our data arising from testing processes and levels of responses varying greatly between countries, our prediction may not be accurate for countries across different stages of the pandemic.
